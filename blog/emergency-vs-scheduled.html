Emergency Plumber vs. Scheduled Repair: When to Call

Not every plumbing issue is an emergency. Here's how to tell the difference — and save yourself the cost of an emergency call-out fee when you don't need one.

🚨 Call NOW (Emergency)

  • Burst pipe with water gushing — shut off the main valve, then call
  • Sewer backup — this is a health hazard, call immediately
  • Complete loss of water to your entire house
  • Overflowing toilet that won't stop, especially on upper floors
  • Gas leak — leave the house and call 911 + your plumber

📅 Can Wait (Scheduled)

  • Dripping faucet — annoying but won't flood your home
  • Running toilet — wastes water but can wait for a regular visit
  • Slow drain — try a plunger or drain snake first
  • Minor leak you can contain with a bucket or towel
  • Water heater not heating — unless there's active leaking

Pro tip: Know where your main water shutoff valve is. In an emergency, turning it off fast can save you thousands in water damage. It's usually near your water meter or where the main line enters your home.

If you're unsure whether it's an emergency, it's better to call and ask. Many Tucson plumbers will talk you through it over the phone for free.
