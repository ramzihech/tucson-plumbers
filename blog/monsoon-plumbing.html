How Monsoon Season Affects Your Plumbing in Tucson

Tucson's monsoon season (June through September) brings heavy rain that can seriously stress your plumbing system. Here's what to watch for and how to prepare.

1. Clogs & Backups

Heavy rain washes debris, sediment, and leaves into your drainage system. Gutters, downspouts, and underground pipes can clog quickly. If you have an older sewer line, the increased water flow can overwhelm it and cause backups into your home.

2. Foundation Movement

The dramatic shift from dry conditions to saturated soil can cause the ground around your foundation to expand and contract. This puts stress on underground pipes, potentially causing slab leaks or burst pipes.

3. Septic System Issues

If you're on a septic system, saturated soil prevents the leach field from draining properly. This can lead to sewage backing up into your home — a serious health hazard.

Preparation Tips

  • Clean gutters and downspouts before monsoon season hits
  • Check exterior drains — make sure they're clear of debris
  • Know your main shutoff valve — you don't want to find it in the dark during a storm
  • Consider a backwater valve if you're prone to sewer backups
  • Have a plumber on speed dial — monsoon issues don't happen during business hours

A little preparation before June goes a long way. If you've had monsoon plumbing issues before, it's worth having a Tucson plumber inspect your system before the rains start.
