Tucson Pipe Repair: Copper vs. PEX

When replacing pipes in a Tucson home, the two main choices are copper and PEX (cross-linked polyethylene). Each has strengths depending on the application.

Copper Pipes

Pros:

  • Extremely durable — can last 50+ years
  • Resistant to UV light — safe for outdoor or exposed applications
  • Fire resistant — won't melt in a fire
  • Natural antimicrobial properties
  • Recyclable

Cons:

  • Expensive — material and labor costs are higher
  • Requires soldering — more labor-intensive installation
  • Can corrode in Tucson's hard water over time
  • Can burst if it freezes

PEX Pipes

Pros:

  • More affordable — material costs are 30-50% less
  • Flexible — installs around obstacles with fewer fittings
  • Resists corrosion and mineral buildup — ideal for hard water
  • Expands rather than bursts when frozen
  • Quieter operation — less water hammer noise

Cons:

  • Not UV-resistant — indoor use only
  • Can be punctured by sharp objects
  • Shorter track record than copper (in use since the 1980s)
  • Requires special fittings and tools

Which is Better for Tucson?

PEX is increasingly the preferred choice for Tucson repiping projects because it resists the mineral buildup that plagues copper in our hard water. For underground repairs or outdoor exposed lines, copper is still the standard.

Many Tucson plumbers will recommend PEX for whole-house repiping and copper for specific outdoor or high-heat applications. Talk to a local pro about what's right for your specific situation.
