3 Signs Your Tucson Home Has Hard Water Damage

Tucson's hard water is loaded with minerals. Over time, they damage your plumbing. Here are the telltale signs:

1. Scale Buildup on Fixtures

White, chalky deposits on faucets and showerheads? That's limescale — calcium and magnesium left behind as water evaporates. It's the most visible sign of hard water.

2. Reduced Water Pressure

Mineral deposits narrow your pipe interiors over time, lowering water flow throughout the house. If your shower used to blast and now trickles, hard water is likely the culprit.

3. Spotty Dishes & Stiff Laundry

Hard water prevents soap from lathering properly. Your dishes come out spotty, your laundry feels stiff, and you're going through detergent faster than you should.

Solution: A water softener. Have your water tested and install one before the damage gets expensive. Most Tucson plumbers can handle this for you.
