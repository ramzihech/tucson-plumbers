Water Heater Maintenance for Arizona Homes

Arizona's hard water is brutal on water heaters. Sediment buildup, mineral deposits, and corrosion happen faster here than in most parts of the country. Regular maintenance can add years to your water heater's life.

1. Drain & Flush the Tank Annually

Sediment settles at the bottom of your water heater tank, reducing efficiency and causing strange rumbling noises. Connect a hose to the drain valve, run it to a floor drain or outside, and flush until the water runs clear. Do this once a year.

2. Check the Anode Rod

The anode rod is a sacrificial metal rod that attracts corrosion so your tank doesn't. In Tucson's hard water, these rods can deplete in 3-5 years instead of the usual 5-7. A heavily corroded rod means your tank is next. Replace it before you need a whole new water heater.

3. Test the T&P Relief Valve

Lift the lever briefly — hot water should discharge. If nothing comes out, or if it continues to drip afterward, the valve needs replacement. This is a critical safety device that prevents pressure buildup from exploding your tank.

4. Set to 120°F

120°F is the sweet spot — hot enough for showers and dishwashing, cool enough to prevent scalding and save on your energy bill. Every 10°F reduction saves 3-5% on water heating costs.

5. Insulate Older Tanks

An insulation blanket on an older water heater reduces standby heat loss. In Arizona's climate, this is less critical than in cold states, but still helps with efficiency, especially if your water heater is in an unconditioned garage.

If you're not comfortable doing this yourself, any Tucson plumber can handle annual water heater maintenance for a reasonable fee. It's cheap insurance against a $1,500+ emergency replacement.
